# Premier League 2026-27: what a market-blind model expects before a ball is kicked

These are Entenser's **preseason priors** for the 2026-27 Premier League — statistical
starting points from team strength, squad value and last season's underlying numbers, before
any 2026-27 match is played. They are priors, not live probabilities: expect them to move
quickly once the season starts. All figures are the model's own simulation output.

The model opens with **Arsenal and Manchester City as a clear two-horse race** — a combined
83% title probability — with Manchester United a distant third. The eye-catching call is
**Liverpool projected fourth on 56.9 points**, with only a 2.3% title share: the model reads
their underlying numbers as having slipped behind the top two. That's exactly the kind of
market-blind disagreement worth watching — if the market prices Liverpool much higher, one of
you is wrong, and the season will tell you which.

## The scramble for Europe

Top-four is where the priors are most uncertain — and therefore most interesting. Below the
big two, five clubs sit in a tight band between roughly 27% and 57% for a Champions League
place. Small swings in early-season form will move these more than any other bucket.

## A caution worth repeating

Preseason relegation and promotion odds carry very little skill until about a quarter of the
season has been played — teams change, and a frozen prior can't see it. Treat these as the
model's opening bid, and check back once real 2026-27 results start updating the numbers.
